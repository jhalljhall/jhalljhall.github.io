About Me

Howdy, friends and neighbors!

I was born in Pikeville, KY in 1974. My parents had a Bluegrass Band and my father was an artist. Even though I grew up in the hills of eastern Kentucky where coal was king I never was interested in that world. I loved the mountains and heritage, but wasn't an outdoors type of person. I graduated from Elkhorn City High School in 1993 and Pikeville College (now UPike) in the summer of 1997. Shortly after graduating from UPike, I discovered an interest in technology, specifically web related technologies and programming.

Actually, my father introduced me to computers much earlier, but I was never interested in green screens and the clunkiness of computers during the 80's. The juice wasn't worth the squeeze for me. I spent my time on the ballfield and in the gym. In 2000, while recovering from back surgery and unemployed, I taught myself how to code. Shortly after I moved to Lexington, Kentucky and started my own web development business, JD Media.

In 2017, I was chosen as one of Fast Company's 100 Most Creative People in Business for teaching a coal miner how to code and helping start Bit Source, a company located in eastern Kentucky that employs former coal miners as coders.

Currently, I teach people to code in Lexington, Kentucky at Awesome Inc as the Lead Instructor for the Web Developer Bootcamp.

I'm also involved in several start ups and entreprenuer efforts across the Commonwealth.
